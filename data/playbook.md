# GetDeal.ai Sales & Go-to-Market Playbook

**Version:** 1.0  
**Date:** January 2026  
**Objective:** Step-by-step guide for sales and GTM execution, focusing on equity transactions (full acquisitions or 20%+ strategic stakes) involving AI companies.

## 1. Platform Positioning

### One-Liner Value Proposition

> **GetDeal.ai is the exclusive, expert-driven marketplace for acquiring and selling high-growth AI companies, connecting premier AI assets with a vetted network of strategic and financial buyers.**

### Selling Side: AI Startups Seeking Exit

- Venture-backed or bootstrapped AI companies
- Typically Seed to Series B stage
- Seeking a full acquisition or significant strategic investment (20%+ stake)
- Founders who value confidentiality, efficiency, and expert guidance

### Buying Side: Qualified Investors & Acquirers

| Buyer Type | Description |
|------------|-------------|
| **Late-Stage VCs** | Seeking exit opportunities for their portfolio or strategic acquisitions |
| **Corporates** | Heads of M&A and Corporate Development at tech and non-tech enterprises seeking to acquire AI technology and talent |
| **Private Equity (PE)** | Tech-focused firms targeting AI companies for platform or add-on acquisitions, typically $1M - $50M range |
| **Family Offices** | Tech-focused offices looking for direct investment opportunities in high-growth AI |
| **Funded Companies** | High-growth companies with recent funding rounds of $5M+ acting as strategic acquirers for technology and talent |

## 2. Seller Acquisition Steps

### 2.1 Identification: Where to Find High-Potential AI Startups

#### VC & Accelerator Portfolios
- Monitor portfolios of top AI-focused VCs (a16z, Sequoia, General Catalyst, Amplify Partners, Zetta Venture Partners)
- Track graduating cohorts from leading accelerators (Y Combinator, Techstars AI)
- Focus on companies 3-5 years post-seed/Series A — common window for considering exit

#### Data Platforms & Signals
- Use Crunchbase and PitchBook to filter for AI companies by funding stage, sector (Generative AI, Healthcare AI, etc.), and employee growth
- Set up alerts for companies that have not raised a new round in 18-24 months — may be exploring alternatives to fundraising

#### Industry Lists & Awards
- Monitor "Top AI Startups" lists from Forbes, CB Insights, The Software Report
- Track winners of AI-specific competitions and awards

#### Founder & Advisor Networks
- Leverage personal networks of GetDeal.ai founding team
- Build relationships with law firms and accounting firms serving tech startups

### 2.2 Outreach Tactics: How to Engage Founders

#### Founder-Led Content Marketing (Inbound)
- Publish high-value blog posts and LinkedIn articles on topics founders care about:
  - "How to Value Your Pre-Revenue AI Startup"
  - "The M&A Playbook for AI Founders"
- Use content to build trust and attract inbound inquiries

#### Warm Introductions
- Request introductions from mutual connections
- VCs who see GetDeal.ai as viable exit path for portfolio

#### Personalized Direct Outreach
- Craft concise, empathetic emails acknowledging their work
- Introduce GetDeal.ai as confidential, founder-friendly alternative to traditional M&A

**Key Messaging Angle:**
> "We provide a private, efficient path to connect with top strategic buyers (like Microsoft, Salesforce) who are specifically looking for AI companies like yours. We handle the process so you can focus on running your business."

### 2.3 Onboarding Process: From First Contact to Live Listing

| Step | Description |
|------|-------------|
| **Step 1: Initial Consultation** | 30-minute confidential call with M&A Advisor to understand founder's goals, company status, and explain platform process |
| **Step 2: Rigorous Vetting** | Company submits preliminary information. GetDeal.ai reviews: Technology (strength/defensibility of AI/IP), Team (caliber of technical/leadership), Traction (PMF, engagement, revenue), Market (size and growth potential) |
| **Step 3: Onboarding Agreement** | Founder signs platform agreement outlining success-fee structure and confidentiality terms |
| **Step 4: Asset Card Creation** | M&A Advisor works with founder to build confidential listing profile |

### 2.4 Asset Card Creation: Building a Compelling Profile

The M&A Advisor assists in preparing:

- **Confidential Teaser:** Anonymized one-paragraph summary
- **Detailed Profile (for approved buyers):**
  - Company overview, problem, and solution
  - Product demo video
  - Key metrics (ARR, user growth, etc.) with integrations to QuickBooks/Xero for verification
  - Technology stack and AI model overview
  - Team bios and structure
  - Financial summary (P&L)
  - Secure data room for sensitive documents

## 3. Buyer Acquisition Steps

### 3A. Late-Stage VCs

**How to Identify & Reach:**
- Use PitchBook and Crunchbase to find VCs active in late-stage (Series C+) AI deals
- Look for partners on boards of multiple AI companies
- Direct, personalized email and LinkedIn outreach to Partners and Principals

**Value Proposition:**
> "We provide a new, efficient exit channel for your AI portfolio companies that are 3-5 years post-Series A. Our curated network of strategic acquirers helps you maximize returns for your LPs without the cost and time of a full investment banking process."

**Outreach Sequence (Apollo.io + LinkedIn):**
- Day 1: Personalized email referencing specific portfolio company, offering GetDeal.ai as exit solution
- Day 2: View their LinkedIn profile
- Day 4: Follow-up email with relevant market insight ("Valuation multiples for AI companies in [sector] are at all-time high")
- Day 7: LinkedIn connection request with personalized note
- Day 10: Final follow-up with soft CTA: "Is portfolio liquidity a priority for you this year?"

### 3B. Corporates (Strategic Buyers)

**Target:** Heads of M&A, Corporate Development, Corporate Strategy at F1000 tech and non-tech companies

**Outreach Approach (Account-Based Marketing):**
- **Identify:** Use LinkedIn Sales Navigator and Grata. Research annual reports and investor calls for AI strategy mentions
- **Personalize:** Craft messaging based on specific strategic needs (entering new market, acquiring AI capability)

**Value Proposition:**
> "GetDeal.ai provides a curated, off-market deal flow of vetted AI companies that align with your strategic priorities. Our platform streamlines technical due diligence, saving your team hundreds of hours and de-risking your acquisition decisions."

**Matched Asset Presentation:**
After initial discovery call, present relevant anonymized asset:
> "Following our conversation, we've identified a generative AI company on our platform with applications in legal tech that seems to be a perfect fit for your product roadmap. I've attached a confidential one-pager for your review."

### 3C. Private Equity Firms

**Target Profiles:**
- Partners and Principals at tech-focused PE firms (Thoma Bravo, Vista Equity Partners, Insight Partners)
- Firms with "buy-and-build" strategies acquiring enterprise software

**Outreach Tactics:**
- Direct email and LinkedIn referencing recent platform acquisitions
- Position GetDeal.ai as source for strategic add-ons

**Value Proposition:**
> "Our platform is a high-signal source for proprietary AI add-on acquisitions for your portfolio companies. We provide vetted targets with strong growth potential and the data you need to build your investment thesis."

**Deal Positioning:** Frame assets in financial metrics — ARR, growth rate, potential for margin expansion

### 3D. Family Offices

**How to Identify:**
- Use PitchBook and specialized directories for family offices with stated interest in direct technology investments
- Network at wealth management and family office conferences

**Approach Strategy:**
- Highly discreet and professional
- Focus on building long-term, trusted relationship
- Emphasize confidentiality and curated nature of opportunities

**Value Proposition:**
> "We provide exclusive, direct access to investment and acquisition opportunities in high-growth, privately-held AI companies, vetted by our team of industry experts."

### 3E. Funded Companies ($5M+ Raised)

**How to Identify:**
- Crunchbase/PitchBook alerts for companies closing Series B or later ($5M+)

**Why They Buy:**
- **Acqui-hire:** Acquire top-tier AI engineering and research talent
- **Technology Acceleration:** Buy technology that would take too long to build
- **Market Expansion:** Acquire company with foothold in new customer segment

**Outreach Approach:**
Target CEO, CFO, or Head of Strategy

**Value Proposition:**
> "Congratulations on your recent funding round. As you look to deploy that capital to accelerate growth, our platform provides access to curated AI teams and technologies that can help you outmaneuver competitors and hit your next milestones faster. Is an acquisition-led growth strategy a priority for you?"

## 4. Matching & Deal Flow Process

### 4.1 Asset Matching Criteria

A match is initiated when vetted seller's profile aligns with qualified buyer's stated acquisition criteria:

| Criterion | Examples |
|-----------|----------|
| Industry/Vertical | Healthcare AI, Fintech AI, Legal AI |
| Technology Stack/Model Type | LLM, Computer Vision, Predictive Analytics |
| Financials | Revenue range, profitability status |
| Deal Size | Buyer's target check size |
| Strategic Rationale | Acqui-hire, Product Integration, Market Entry |

### 4.2 Sending Matched Assets

| Step | Action |
|------|--------|
| **Step 1: Internal Review** | M&A Advisor identifies potential match |
| **Step 2: Prepare One-Pager** | Confidential 1-2 page PDF with anonymized overview, key metrics, tech highlights, and "Why this is a fit for [Buyer]" section |
| **Step 3: Confirm NDA** | Ensure buyer has signed NDA on file |
| **Step 4: Personalized Send** | Advisor sends one-pager via email with personalized rationale. Use DocSend to monitor engagement |

### 4.3 Follow-up Sequence

Managed via Pipedrive tasks:

- **Day 3:** "Hi [Buyer], just wanted to follow up and see if you had any initial thoughts on the [Asset Type] company I sent over. Happy to answer any preliminary questions."
- **Day 7:** "Hi [Buyer], checking in one last time. If timing isn't right, just let me know. If you'd like to learn more, we can set up a brief introductory call with the founder next week."
- **Day 14:** If no response, mark as "On Hold" — re-engage in 3-6 months

### 4.4 Deal Facilitation

| Step | Description |
|------|-------------|
| **Step 1: Introductory Call** | M&A Advisor facilitates confidential intro call between buyer and seller's founder |
| **Step 2: Data Room Access** | Following successful call, buyer granted access to secure data room for deeper DD |
| **Step 3: LOI & Negotiation** | Advisor assists both parties in navigating Letter of Intent and negotiation, acting as neutral intermediary |
| **Step 4: Closing** | Advisor works with legal and financial teams on both sides to guide deal to successful close |

## 5. GTM Execution Steps

### 5.1 LinkedIn Operations

#### Corporate Page Setup
- Professional banner with tagline: "The Marketplace for AI Acquisitions"
- SEO-optimized "About" section detailing value prop for buyers and sellers
- CTA button: "Learn More" → platform contact page
- Content calendar: 3-4 posts/week (anonymized deal announcements, market data infographics, reshares of founder content)

#### 3 Founders Positioning

| Founder | Focus | Content Themes |
|---------|-------|----------------|
| **Founder 1 (AI Expert)** | "AI Technology & Due Diligence" | Assessing AI tech, data moats, IP risks |
| **Founder 2 (M&A Expert)** | "AI M&A and Valuation" | Deal structures, negotiation, financial modeling. Publishes quarterly market report |
| **Founder 3 (Tech Visionary)** | "Building the Future of M&A" | GetDeal.ai product vision, how technology transforms deal-making |

#### Direct Outreach Cadence (Manual & Personalized)
- **Daily Limit:** 20-25 new personalized connection requests per team member
- **Connection Note:** "Hi [First Name], I saw your work at [Company] and was impressed. We're focused on AI acquisitions at GetDeal.ai and seeing some interesting assets that align with your space. Would be great to connect."

**Follow-up Sequence (if connection accepted):**
- Day 1: Thank them, state core value prop
- Day 4: Soft follow-up with relevant insight or question
- Day 10: Polite "breakup" message if no response

### 5.2 Email Operations (Apollo + Pipedrive)

#### Apollo.io Setup
- Build lead lists by ICP criteria ("Head of M&A" at "SaaS companies" with "500+ employees")
- Enrich contact data for verified emails
- Create multi-step, multi-channel sequences (Email + LinkedIn tasks)

#### Pipedrive Setup
- Pipeline stages: `Lead In → Contact Made → Needs Discovery → Asset Matched → LOI Submitted → Due Diligence → Closed-Won / Closed-Lost`
- Automation for follow-up tasks when deal moves stages
- Integrate with Apollo for automatic deal creation on positive reply

#### Sequence Templates

**Sequence 1: Cold Outreach to Corporate M&A**
- Step 1 (Day 1, Email): Subject: "AI Acquisition pipeline for [Company Name]" — Focus on efficiency and de-risking
- Step 2 (Day 2, LinkedIn): View prospect's profile
- Step 3 (Day 4, Email): Reply thread, add specific data point about M&A in their industry
- Step 4 (Day 7, LinkedIn): Send connection request
- Step 5 (Day 10, Email): Final follow-up with soft CTA

**Sequence 2: Outreach to Funded Company CEO**
- Step 1 (Day 1, Email): Subject: "Accelerating your roadmap post-funding" — Focus on speed and competitive advantage
- Step 2 (Day 3, Email): Follow-up with link to case study or "acqui-hiring" article
- Step 3 (Day 6, LinkedIn): Engage with recent post from prospect
- Step 4 (Day 9, Email): Final email asking if acquisition-led growth is current priority

### 5.3 Pipeline Management (Pipedrive)

- All active conversations must live in Pipedrive
- Every deal must have "Next Activity" scheduled to prevent leads going cold
- Use deal "Rotting" feature to flag inactive deals
- **Mandatory weekly pipeline review meeting** to discuss deal progression and strategy

## 6. Unit Economics Summary

| Metric | Description | Target Value |
|--------|-------------|--------------|
| **Revenue Model** | Hybrid: Buyer Subscriptions + Seller Success Fees | — |
| **Buyer Subscriptions** | Annual fees for platform access | Premium: $10,000/yr, Platinum: $25,000/yr |
| **Seller Success Fees** | Tiered commission on final deal value | 7% up to $1M, 5% from $1M-$10M, 3% above $10M |
| **Target Deal Sizes** | Range of transaction values | $2M - $50M+ |
| **Average Take Rate** | Blended commission as % of GMV | ~5-7% |
| **CAC (Blended)** | Fully loaded cost to acquire one customer | $7,500 (initial target) |
| **LTV (Platinum Buyer)** | Gross profit from single Platinum buyer | $80,000 (4-yr lifespan) |
| **LTV (High-Value Seller)** | Gross profit from single $5M transaction | $216,000 |
| **LTV:CAC Ratio** | Customer lifetime value to acquisition cost | > 7:1 |
